## 1. Purpose

This document maps the foundational corpus of the long care — the federation committed to multi-decadal restoration of the biosphere — and of Robotanica, the founding company that built the corpus and the platforms supporting it.

It exists to answer:

- What documents exist?
- How do they relate to each other?
- In what order should I read them?
- Where do I find the canonical statement on a specific topic?
- Which documents depend on which?
- What is the status of each?
- What open questions cut across the corpus?

The corpus comprises **thirty-five substantive documents** plus **this Index** plus **the manifesto in two languages** (English: *the long care*; Spanish: *el cuidado largo*).

This Index is a substantial revision of v0. The corpus has nearly doubled since v0 was written.

## 2. Naming and Structure

### 2.1 Two-Name Architecture

The project has two names, deliberately distinct:

**Robotanica** — the founding company. The legal entity that:
- Holds founding capital and operates initially.
- Builds and operates the technical platforms (Gaia, A3, OpenFactory).
- Employs the founding team.
- Stewards the protocol during the founding period.
- Develops the technology stack.

**The long care** (English) — the federation, the mission, the multi-decadal commitment. The federation that:
- Communities, operators, scientists, and others join.
- Tends the trillion-tree commitment.
- Operates across regions and cultures.
- Outlives any single organization.
- Is named in each language by the words that carry the meaning.

**Multilingual federation naming.** The federation is named in each language by that language's own words for its meaning. Examples:

- English: *the long care*
- Spanish: *el cuidado largo*
- Portuguese: *o cuidado longo* (suggested)
- French: *le long soin* (suggested)
- Mandarin: 长养 / 久护 (suggested)
- Other languages: determined by communities of speakers, with their consent

Every language's name is the federation's name. None is canonical; all are co-equal.

### 2.2 Document Names

Documents in the corpus reflect the naming distinction:

- **Robotanica documents** specify the company's structures (e.g., the Founder Charter applies to Robotanica's founders).
- **Federation documents** specify the federation (e.g., the Charter belongs to the federation, not to Robotanica).
- **Cross-cutting documents** specify shared substrate (e.g., the Protocol Specifications govern federation, but were authored by Robotanica during founding).

Where a document was authored as "Robotanica's Charter" or similar, the substantive content belongs to the federation. The name "Robotanica" attaches to specific aspects of the founding period.

In documents written before this naming distinction was made, the name "Robotanica" is used in both senses. Operational practice clarifies the meaning per context.

## 4. Layered Architecture

The corpus is layered. Higher layers govern lower layers; lower layers operationalize higher layers.

### 4.1 Layer 1 — Constitutional and Manifesto

**The Charter.** The founding document. Articulates mission, principles, the Steward's Covenant, governance, openness commitments, federation, failure protocols, and amendment. Designates certain provisions as un-amendable.

**The Manifesto.** Public declaration of the federation's commitments, in plain language across multiple languages. Stand-alone, multi-language, intended for public reading.

### 4.2 Layer 2 — Federation Contract

**Protocol Specifications.** Cross-platform federation contract. Implementation-agnostic, testable, stable. What anyone must implement to participate as a peer. What enables forking per Charter §5.4.

### 4.3 Layer 3 — Strategic Synthesis

**White Paper.** Synthesizes the Charter into a strategic document for broader audiences. The on-ramp document.

### 4.4 Layer 4 — Platform Engineering

**Gaia PRD, A3 PRD, OpenFactory PRD.** Three platform engineering specifications. Reference implementations.

**Technical Architecture Plan.** Engineering layer beneath the PRDs. Service decomposition, data models, deployment topology.

### 4.5 Layer 5 — Governance Instruments

**Stewardship Lease Requirements, Federation Membership Agreement, Science Council Charter, AI Ethical Review Framework, Founder Charter.** Documents that constitute specific governance bodies, instruments, and processes.

### 4.6 Layer 6 — Mission Disciplines

**MRV Specification, Threat Model, Funding Strategy, Bioregional Strategy, Personnel and Compensation Philosophy, Genetic Strategy Position Paper.** Documents specifying how the mission is conducted with rigor.

### 4.7 Layer 7 — Operational Specifications

**Data Lifecycle and Preservation Plan, Public Communication Doctrine, Insurance and Liability Architecture, Dependencies and Partnerships Map, Onboarding and Capacity-Building Specification.** Operational disciplines.

### 4.8 Layer 8 — Strategic Operational

**Unit Economics and Costing Model, Theory of Change, Pilot Operation Protocol, Demand-Side and Credit Market Strategy, Climate Scenario Assumptions, Plurality of Knowledge Operational Protocol.** Documents bridging strategy to action.

### 4.9 Layer 9 — Strategic Engagement

**Regulatory Engagement Strategy.** Engagement with policy and regulatory ecosystems.

**Founding Capital Plan.** Operational fundraising plan complementing the Funding Strategy.

### 4.10 Layer 10 — Vision and Brand

**Vision Document, Brand and Visual Identity Brief.** Coordination through imagination and visual presence.

## 9. Status, Versioning, and Amendment

### 9.1 Current Status

All documents are at v0 (founding) drafts except this Index (v1). None has been ratified. The corpus is presented as a coherent first complete pass.

### 9.2 Per-Document Amendment

- **Charter** — most rigorous: 180 days posting, separate approvals from Federation governance, Science Council, and Stewardship Office; un-amendable provisions per §8.1.
- **Manifesto** — public document. Substantive amendments rare. Multilingual versions developed independently per language by their communities.
- **Protocol Specifications** — major version changes require 18-month parallel operation.
- **Science Council Charter** and **AI Ethical Review Framework** — own un-amendable provisions; review every 3-10 years.
- **Mission disciplines** (MRV, Funding, Bioregional, Personnel, Genetic Strategy) — five-year comprehensive review; Threat Model annually.
- **PRDs** — engineering documents; revised as platforms evolve, version-controlled.
- **Operational specifications** — five-year comprehensive review.
- **Stewardship Lease Requirements** — jurisdictional variants drafted from this requirements spec.
- **Federation Membership Agreement** — amendments require consultation with affected member categories.
- **Founder Charter** — template adapted by specific founders.
- **White Paper, Vision Document, Brand Brief** — revised as strategy evolves.
- **Index** — updated when documents are added.

### 9.3 Cross-Document Coherence

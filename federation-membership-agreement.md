# Federation Membership Agreement

## Requirements Specification (v0)

**Document type:** Requirements specification for a federation governance instrument
**Status:** Draft for ratification
**Companion documents:** Robotanica Charter, Protocol Specifications, A3 PRD, Science Council Charter

---

## 1. Purpose and Status

This document specifies the Federation Membership Agreement: the instrument under which operators, scientists, fabricators, partner organizations, verifiers, and other parties enter the Robotanica federation.

The Charter (§4.4 and §6) names the Federation as the structural form of Robotanica and admits external parties as members under its terms. This document specifies what those terms are: what membership requires, what it grants, how it is gained, maintained, and ended.

Like the Stewardship Lease Requirements, this is **not** the legal agreement itself. It is the requirements specification from which member-category-specific and jurisdiction-specific agreements are drafted. The actual signed instruments will vary by member category, by jurisdiction, and by the scope of activity, but all must satisfy what is specified here.

---

## 2. Membership Categories

The Federation admits members in distinct categories, each with category-specific commitments and rights.

### 2.1 Operators

**Operators** are federation members that conduct day-to-day stewardship operations on parcels: planting, tending, monitoring, biological production, and field operations. Operators may be commercial entities, cooperatives, NGOs, or individuals operating at the scale that warrants direct membership.

### 2.2 Verifiers

**Verifiers** are accredited bodies that perform independent verification of restoration outcomes per the MRV Specification. Verifiers must satisfy the independence requirements of MRV §3.3.

### 2.3 Scientific Contributors

**Scientific Contributors** are individuals or institutions contributing to Gaia's interpretation layer: species records, recipe authoring and revision, ecological research, methodological development.

### 2.4 Fabricators

**Fabricators** produce components, robots, sensors, biological inputs, and other physical artifacts for OpenFactory's reference operations or for federation-wide use. Subject to supply-chain attestation requirements.

### 2.5 Partner Organizations

**Partner Organizations** are mission-aligned organizations that interoperate with Robotanica's federation while maintaining their own primary identity: ecosystem restoration camps, regenerative agriculture cooperatives, indigenous land councils, conservation NGOs, restoration foundations, and others. Partner Organizations operate under bilateral terms that reference but do not subsume their own missions.

### 2.6 Funders

**Funders** are members providing capital under defined terms, subject to the Charter's funding principles (§3.6). Funder membership is distinct from operating membership: funders do not gain operational rights through funding.

### 2.7 Researchers

**Researchers** are academic institutions or independent researchers accessing Federation data and platforms for non-commercial scientific work. Researcher membership is structured to support open science while protecting sovereignty constraints.

### 2.8 Civil Society Participants

**Civil Society Participants** is a lighter membership category for individuals and organizations engaging with Robotanica's public processes, reviewing the public record, raising concerns, and participating in consultation. This category exists to ensure that participation in Federation governance is not limited to those with operational or financial stakes.

### 2.9 Cross-Category Membership

A single party may hold multiple membership categories where roles do not conflict. A scientist may also be a research-focused member; an operator may also be a fabricator. Where roles create conflict (e.g., an operator also serving as a verifier on their own work), category-specific independence requirements prevail.

## 3. Universal Commitments

All members, regardless of category, commit to:

### 3.1 Charter Conformance

**MA-101** Members commit to honor the Robotanica Charter in the work conducted under the Federation's protocols. Specific Charter provisions of particular relevance:

- §2 (Principles), including the long horizon, ecology leads / engineering serves, stewardship not ownership, evidence over advocacy, plurality of knowledge, transparent failure, and subsidiarity.
- §3 (Steward's Covenant), in the scope relevant to the member's work.
- §5 (Open Source and Protocol), including the openness commitments and forkability.

### 3.2 Protocol Conformance

**MA-102** Members commit to satisfy the Protocol Specifications at the conformance level applicable to their category.

**MA-103** Members commit to participate in conformance testing and recertification per Protocol §13.

### 3.3 Sovereignty Honor

**MA-104** Members commit to honor sovereignty declarations associated with the data, parcels, and entities they interact with, per Protocol §10 and the CARE Principles where applicable.

### 3.4 Indigenous and Local Recognition

**MA-105** Members commit to FPIC where their work involves indigenous or local communities, per Charter §3.3 and the Lease Requirements §3.

**MA-106** Members commit to refrain from work that compromises indigenous or community sovereignty, regardless of legal title arrangements in the relevant jurisdiction.

### 3.5 Transparent Failure

**MA-107** Members commit to report failures, errors, and mistakes within the scope of their work, per Charter §2.9 and the MRV reconciliation discipline.

**MA-108** Members commit not to conceal information that would be material to the public record, the Federation, or affected parties.

### 3.6 Conflict Disclosure

**MA-109** Members commit to disclose material conflicts of interest at the time they arise, per category-specific requirements.

### 3.7 Data Stewardship

**MA-110** Members commit to handle data accessed through the Federation per the access terms of that data, including sovereignty constraints, retention requirements, and confidentiality where applicable.

### 3.8 Cooperation in Disputes and Investigations

**MA-111** Members commit to cooperate with Federation investigations, dispute proceedings, and audits within the scope of their membership and work.

### 3.9 Non-Retaliation

**MA-112** Members commit not to retaliate against whistleblowers, dispute initiators, or parties exercising legitimate participation rights.

## 4. Category-Specific Commitments

Beyond universal commitments, each category carries specific obligations.

### 4.1 Operators

In addition to universal commitments, Operators commit to:

**MA-201** Faithfully execute recipes from Gaia, with deviations recorded as operational events.

**MA-202** Maintain the genealogy and traceability requirements of OpenFactory (§6.5 OF-O-006).

**MA-203** Honor Service Contracts as specified in A3, including timely completion, quality, and reporting.

**MA-204** Protect worker safety per applicable jurisdictional regulation and per Robotanica's safety baseline.

**MA-205** Honor the no-abandonment provision (Lease Requirements §6.6): Operators may not leave a parcel in a partially-restored state without succession or explicit closeout.

**MA-206** Submit to performance review by the Federation: completion rates, outcome quality, dispute rates, and verification outcomes are tracked.

**MA-207** Refrain from anti-competitive behavior including bid collusion, market manipulation, and Sybil-pattern behaviors in the marketplace.

### 4.2 Verifiers

In addition to universal commitments, Verifiers commit to:

**MA-301** Verifier independence per MRV §3.3: no financial interest in verified outcomes; no current or recent (two-year cooling-off) employment by the Operators they verify.

**MA-302** Verification methodology conforming to the published MRV Specification at the relevant level.

**MA-303** Submission to cross-verification per MRV §7.3 as part of the verifier accountability discipline.

**MA-304** Public posting of verification reports per MRV §7.4 (subject to sovereignty and PII protections).

**MA-305** Refrain from cozy long-term operator-verifier relationships that would compromise independence; verifier rotation per MRV §7.5.

### 4.3 Scientific Contributors

In addition to universal commitments, Scientific Contributors commit to:

**MA-401** Submission of contributions through the proposal-review-merge protocol of Gaia's interpretation layer.

**MA-402** Citation discipline: linking claims to evidence, attributing prior work, distinguishing one's own work from others' work.

**MA-403** Refrain from gatekeeping or capture of the codex through coordinated activity.

**MA-404** Disclosure of funding sources and institutional ties for any contribution.

**MA-405** Where contributions overlap with traditional ecological knowledge, attribution and collaboration with the relevant indigenous and community knowledge holders, on terms set by those holders.

### 4.4 Fabricators

In addition to universal commitments, Fabricators commit to:

**MA-501** Supply chain attestation per Threat Model §5.3: verified component sourcing, tamper-evident records, supply-chain integrity audit access.

**MA-502** Open hardware practices where compatible with their business model: published interfaces, documented designs for components used in restoration work, refusal to embed proprietary lock-in in critical components.

**MA-503** Compliance with biosecurity protocols where producing biological inputs.

**MA-504** Disposal and recycling discipline for end-of-life components, per applicable environmental regulations and Federation guidance.

### 4.5 Partner Organizations

In addition to universal commitments, Partner Organizations commit to:

**MA-601** Mission compatibility: the Partner Organization's mission, while distinct from Robotanica's, is compatible with Charter principles. Where missions diverge in specific areas, the divergence is acknowledged and managed bilaterally.

**MA-602** Bilateral terms specifying the scope of cooperation, data sharing, joint operations, and dispute resolution.

**MA-603** Refrain from using the Federation relationship to legitimize practices the Charter would refuse.

### 4.6 Funders

In addition to universal commitments, Funders commit to:

**MA-701** Compliance with the Charter's funding principles (§3.6): refusal of conditions that would compromise mission or principles.

**MA-702** Public disclosure of the funding relationship, in summary form sufficient for stakeholders to assess any influence.

**MA-703** Acceptance that funding does not grant operational, governance, or selection rights beyond those specifically agreed.

**MA-704** Refrain from leveraging funding to influence Council, Stewardship Office, or platform decisions.

### 4.7 Researchers

In addition to universal commitments, Researchers commit to:

**MA-801** Honor data sovereignty constraints; researcher access to Federation data does not override sovereignty declarations.

**MA-802** Open publication where compatible with sovereignty (open-access preferred for non-sovereignty-restricted data).

**MA-803** Citation of Federation data sources per academic norms; data access does not transfer ownership.

**MA-804** Refrain from research that violates ethical standards or that would harm subjects (parcels, communities, ecosystems).

### 4.8 Civil Society Participants

Civil Society Participants commit to:

**MA-901** Participation in good faith in public consultation, comment processes, and dispute mechanisms where they have standing.

**MA-902** Refrain from disruptive participation patterns: spam, harassment, attempts to manipulate processes through coordinated bad-faith activity.

## 5. Universal Rights

All members, regardless of category, hold the following rights:

### 5.1 Access

**MA-1001** Access to the Federation's protocol specifications, reference implementations, and public record at the granularity their category warrants.

### 5.2 Participation

**MA-1002** Participation in Federation governance at the level of their work, per nested governance (Charter §4.5).

### 5.3 Withdrawal

**MA-1003** Right to withdraw from the Federation under defined terms (§8 below), with retention of their own data and the data they have rights to.

### 5.4 Forking

**MA-1004** Right to fork the Federation's protocols and platforms per Charter §5.4. Forking is not retaliated against; members in good standing remain in good standing for the period during which they participate, regardless of forking activities.

### 5.5 Due Process

**MA-1005** Due process in any Federation discipline: notice of complaint, opportunity to respond, hearing where applicable, defined appeal processes.

### 5.6 Non-Discrimination

**MA-1006** Federation operations do not discriminate on the basis of category beyond what is structurally necessary, geographic origin, gender, or other protected characteristics.

### 5.7 Whistleblower Protection

**MA-1007** Members raising concerns through proper channels are protected from retaliation per Charter §3.5.

### 5.8 Voice in Governance Evolution

**MA-1008** Members have voice in Federation governance evolution, including this Membership Agreement, through the amendment processes of the relevant documents.

## 6. Category-Specific Rights

### 6.1 Operators

Beyond universal rights, Operators have the right to:

- Participate in the marketplace per A3 §6.3.
- Performance history reflected accurately in Federation matching processes.
- Operator selection consent rights (Lease Requirements §6.5 succession).
- Compensation for work completed per Service Contracts.

### 6.2 Verifiers

Beyond universal rights, Verifiers have the right to:

- Access to the data necessary for verification of their accredited scope.
- Independent operation: Federation may not direct findings.
- Compensation for verification work, on terms that preserve independence.

### 6.3 Scientific Contributors

Beyond universal rights, Scientific Contributors have the right to:

- Authorship attribution on their contributions to Gaia.
- Open peer review of their submissions.
- Defense against retaliatory rejection of well-founded contributions.

### 6.4 Fabricators

Beyond universal rights, Fabricators have the right to:

- Specifications and standards published openly.
- Fair sourcing processes (no preferential treatment for incumbents beyond what performance and quality justify).

### 6.5 Partner Organizations

Beyond universal rights, Partner Organizations have the right to:

- Bilateral coordination respecting their independent mission.
- Recognition of their primary identity and brand.
- Terms-specified data sharing reciprocity.

### 6.6 Funders

Beyond universal rights, Funders have the right to:

- Reporting on the use of their funds at appropriate granularity.
- Audit access proportionate to their funding scope.
- Recognition of their support, in forms compatible with §6.6 of the Charter (transparent attribution, not branding override).

### 6.7 Researchers

Beyond universal rights, Researchers have the right to:

- Access to non-sovereignty-restricted Federation data for research purposes.
- Use of Federation platforms for non-commercial research.
- Publication of research findings.

### 6.8 Civil Society Participants

Beyond universal rights, Civil Society Participants have the right to:

- Standing to file complaints and participate in consultation per defined processes.
- Access to the public record.
- Participation in Council and Federation review processes where their categories of participation are defined.

## 7. Admission

### 7.1 Application

A party seeking membership submits an application specifying:

- The category or categories of membership sought
- Identity and credentials appropriate to the category
- Disclosures relevant to the category (financial interests, institutional ties)
- Acknowledgment of universal and category-specific commitments

### 7.2 Review

Applications are reviewed by the Federation governance body or its delegated committee. Review covers:

- Conformance test results (per Protocol §13)
- Disclosure adequacy
- Mission alignment
- Capacity for ongoing commitment

### 7.3 Decision

Admission decisions are recorded as `GovernanceDecision` entities (per A3 §6.7). Approvals, conditional approvals, and rejections are all recorded with reasoning.

### 7.4 Appeal

Rejected applicants may appeal the decision, with the appeal reviewed by an appeal body distinct from the original reviewers.

### 7.5 Public Record

Admission events are public per Protocol §10.4. The public record includes the applicant, the category, the decision, and (in summary form) the reasoning.

## 8. Withdrawal

### 8.1 Voluntary Withdrawal

A member may withdraw voluntarily at any time by formal notice. Withdrawal becomes effective on a date no earlier than 90 days from notice (or sooner if the member's role permits clean immediate exit).

### 8.2 Withdrawal Obligations

Withdrawing members must:

- Complete or transfer outstanding work commitments per applicable Service Contracts and Leases.
- Honor data sovereignty obligations for data they hold.
- Return or properly dispose of Federation-controlled assets.
- Cooperate with successor arrangements where their work continues under another operator.

### 8.3 Member Rights on Withdrawal

Withdrawing members retain:

- Their own data, in open formats per Protocol INV-020.
- Data they have rights to (e.g., parcel data per their Lease relationships, where applicable).
- Right to operate independently of the Federation, including the right to fork.

### 8.4 No Penalty for Withdrawal

Withdrawal in good standing does not carry penalty. Members withdraw in good standing if they have honored their obligations through the effective withdrawal date.

### 8.5 Re-admission

A withdrawn member may re-apply for membership. Re-admission follows the standard admission process; prior membership is a relevant factor but not determinative.

## 9. Discipline

### 9.1 Graduated Sanctions

Per Charter §6.4 and Ostrom's fifth principle, sanctions are graduated:

- **Notice of concern** — formal notification of conduct that may warrant discipline; opportunity to remedy.
- **Censure** — formal recorded condemnation without operational impact.
- **Conditional standing** — continued membership conditional on remedial action; missed conditions trigger escalation.
- **Suspension** — temporary loss of operational rights (marketplace dispatch, scientific contribution, etc.); duration and conditions for reinstatement specified.
- **Expulsion** — termination of membership.

### 9.2 Grounds for Discipline

Discipline may be triggered by:

- Material breach of universal or category-specific commitments
- Conformance failure not remedied
- Conduct violating the Charter
- Conduct harming other members, parties, or ecosystems
- Patterns of conduct that, individually minor, collectively undermine the Federation

### 9.3 Process

Disciplinary processes proceed through:

- **Complaint** — filed by Federation members, governance bodies, or affected parties
- **Investigation** — by an investigator with no conflict of interest in the matter
- **Notice and hearing** — the affected member receives notice, evidence, and opportunity to respond
- **Decision** — by the appropriate governance body, with reasoning recorded
- **Public record** — the decision and reasoning are public; personal information protected as appropriate

### 9.4 Appeal

Disciplinary decisions may be appealed. Appeal proceeds to a body distinct from the original decision-maker.

### 9.5 Restorative Approach

Discipline preferences restoration where possible. Expulsion is last resort. Members who acknowledge and remedy harm are preferred to be restored to good standing.

### 9.6 Effects of Suspension and Expulsion

Suspension terminates operational rights for the duration. Active commitments must be transferred to other members or wound down without harm to affected parties (Lease relationships, contracts, etc.).

Expulsion terminates membership. Active commitments are subject to succession or wind-down per A3 §6.2 and Lease Requirements §6.5.

### 9.7 No Ban on Forking

Suspension or expulsion from the Federation does not bar the affected party from forking Federation protocols and operating independently per Charter §5.4. Federation discipline does not extend to legitimate independent operation outside Federation conformance.

## 10. Disputes Among Members

### 10.1 Direct Resolution

Members in dispute attempt direct resolution first, per the principle of resolution at the lowest competent level.

### 10.2 Federation-Mediated Resolution

Where direct resolution fails, members may invoke Federation mediation through A3's dispute protocols (A3 §6.8).

### 10.3 External Forums

Where Federation processes do not resolve the dispute, members may pursue external forums per applicable jurisdictional rules and the dispute terms in their specific category agreements.

### 10.4 Indigenous Forums

Where the dispute involves indigenous parties, indigenous customary forums take precedence if invoked by the indigenous party (Lease Requirements §12.4).

---

## 11. Term and Renewal

### 11.1 Indefinite Term

Membership is indefinite, subject to:

- Voluntary withdrawal (§8)
- Discipline through suspension or expulsion (§9)
- Recertification (§11.3)

### 11.2 Annual Standing Confirmation

Members annually confirm their continued commitment and disclose any material changes (institutional ties, conflict of interest, capacity changes).

### 11.3 Recertification

Per Protocol §13.3, members recertify on a defined cadence (default: annual) or upon protocol version upgrade. Failure to recertify results in suspension pending compliance.

### 11.4 No Automatic Renewal of Inactive Membership

Members who have not exercised their membership in a defined period (default: three years) are subject to inactive-status review and possible administrative termination.

---

## 12. Member Sovereignty and Federation Authority

### 12.1 Member Sovereignty

Federation members retain their own governance, mission, and decision-making within their organizations. Federation membership does not subsume member identity or override member governance.

### 12.2 Federation Authority

Federation authority over members is bounded by the commitments of this Agreement and the protocols. Federation may not direct member internal decisions outside the scope of Federation work.

### 12.3 Bilateral Limits

Some categories (Partner Organizations, large institutional members) operate under bilateral terms in addition to this Agreement. Bilateral terms refine but do not weaken this Agreement.

### 12.4 Overlapping Memberships in Other Federations

Members may belong to other federations, networks, or coalitions. Such overlapping memberships are permitted; potential conflicts (e.g., compatibility with other federations' commitments) are managed through disclosure.

---

## 13. Forking and Member Status

### 13.1 Forking Per Charter §5.4

Members may, individually or collectively, fork the Federation's protocols and platforms. Forking is a structural right and is not grounds for discipline.

### 13.2 Member Status During Fork

A member who participates in a fork may:

- Continue Federation membership while operating in the fork (parallel operation, where the member's Federation work and fork work are clearly distinct).
- Withdraw from the Federation to operate fully in the fork.

### 13.3 Sovereignty Inheritance

A fork inherits sovereignty obligations on data carried over (Protocol §10.5).

### 13.4 No Competitive Penalty

The Federation does not penalize members for forking decisions. Where members operate well in both Federation and fork capacities, both are legitimate.

---

## 14. Amendment

### 14.1 Subordinate to Charter and Protocol

This Agreement is subordinate to the main Charter and the Protocol Specifications. Conflicts are resolved in favor of the Charter (then the Protocol). This Agreement may not amend Charter un-amendable provisions.

### 14.2 Amendment Process

Amendments to this Agreement may be proposed by:

- The Federation governance body
- The Stewardship Office
- The Science Council (for amendments touching scientific contribution categories)
- Any group of members in good standing meeting a defined threshold (e.g., 5% of members or 50 members, whichever is greater)

Amendments require:

- Public posting for at least 90 days
- Consultation with affected member categories
- Approval by the Federation governance body
- Notification of all members

### 14.3 Provisions Designated Stable

The following provisions of this Agreement are designated stable; amendments require additional consultation and a higher approval threshold:

- §3 Universal Commitments
- §5 Universal Rights, particularly §5.4 Forking
- §8 Withdrawal rights
- §13 Forking provisions

## 15. Adoption

### 15.1 Founding Adoption

This Agreement takes effect upon ratification by the Federation governance body. Founding members adopt by signature.

### 15.2 Subsequent Adoption

Members admitted after founding adopt by signing the Agreement at admission.

### 15.3 Public Posting

This Agreement is posted publicly, in open formats, in the Federation's languages.

## 16. Open Questions

These are unresolved issues that the founding Federation will address as the Agreement operates:

- **Member-class admission limits.** Whether some categories should have admission caps to preserve quality (e.g., verifier numbers managed to maintain expertise standards) or remain open is unresolved.
- **Cross-jurisdictional category-specific terms.** Operator agreements in different jurisdictions may need substantially different specific terms; how variations are managed without fragmenting the federation is unresolved.
- **Civil society participation thresholds.** What level of standing civil society participants have in formal governance (beyond comment) is not yet specified.
- **Indigenous participation as members.** Indigenous communities are involved as Acknowledged Parties under Leases (Lease Requirements §3.2) and through Council seats. Whether indigenous communities also enter as Federation members per this Agreement, or operate exclusively through other mechanisms, needs resolution.
- **Funder influence beyond direct rights.** The Charter and this Agreement bound funder rights, but indirect influence (relationship effects, expectation management) is harder to specify. Operational practices will need to evolve.
- **Member discipline jurisdictional coordination.** When a member operates across jurisdictions and faces discipline, coordination across the relevant legal systems is not yet specified in detail.
- **Cross-federation operability.** Where Robotanica's federation interoperates with other federations (regenerative agriculture networks, conservation alliances), the membership status of cross-federation members is not yet specified.
- **Public participation as "membership" or as something else.** Civil Society Participation may be better structured as a non-membership form of participation. The boundary between member and non-member participation needs review.

## Appendix C — Glossary (Membership-Specific)

- **Civil Society Participant** — A lighter-membership category for individuals and organizations engaging with public processes.
- **Conformance level** — Read, Write, or Platform conformance per Protocol §13.1.
- **Cross-category membership** — A single party holding multiple membership categories.
- **Federation governance body** — The Federation-wide governance entity referenced in the main Charter and this Agreement.
- **Fork** — Independent operation of Federation protocols/platforms per Charter §5.4.
- **Good standing** — Active membership status without active discipline or compliance failure.
- **Member category** — One of the defined categories of Federation membership (Operator, Verifier, Scientific Contributor, Fabricator, Partner Organization, Funder, Researcher, Civil Society Participant).
- **Recertification** — Periodic conformance reverification per Protocol §13.3.

# Personnel and Compensation Philosophy

## Specification (v0)

**Document type:** Strategic and governance specification
**Status:** Draft for ratification
**Companion documents:** Robotanica Charter, Funding Strategy, Threat Model, Science Council Charter, Federation Membership Agreement

---

## 1. Purpose and Status

This document specifies how Robotanica treats the people who do its work. It defines:

- The principles binding hiring, compensation, working conditions, and protection.
- The compensation framework, including how compensation varies across regions, roles, and tenures.
- The structural provisions protecting personnel safety, dissent, and whistleblowing.
- The governance of personnel decisions.

Charter §3.5 commits Robotanica to its people: fair pay including those whose labor occurs in the field and through long quiet years of monitoring; refusal to demand sacrifice of life and health; protection of dissent. The Funding Strategy (§8.4) and Threat Model (§4.11, §4.12) both reference this document. The Science Council Charter (§6.6) builds on its compensation framework.

The document operationalizes commitments. The Charter is the principle layer; this is how the principles become payroll, conditions, and safeguards.

A note on tone. This is a compensation philosophy, not a compensation policy. Specific pay bands, role definitions, and procedures live in operational documents that change frequently. This document specifies what does not change: the principles, the structural protections, the floor below which Robotanica will not go.

---

## 2. Why This Document Matters

Multi-decade work is done by people. The work outlives any single person, but it cannot continue without people. The conditions under which they work — pay, safety, dignity, security, ability to dissent — determine whether the work continues to be done well or whether it decays into compliance.

Most mission-driven organizations either:

- Underpay their people in the name of mission, demanding sacrifice that compounds into burnout, attrition, and exclusion of those who cannot afford to sacrifice, or
- Replicate extractive compensation structures (founder windfalls, executive multiples, equity lotteries) that contradict the mission they claim.

Robotanica must avoid both. Fair compensation is not a luxury that arrives once mission is established. It is a structural condition for the mission to survive.

The Threat Model named several personnel-adjacent risks: insider threats (§4.11), threats to people (§4.12), founder capture (§3.7). The defenses for those risks include personnel structures designed to avoid the conditions in which they manifest.

## 3. Principles

These principles bind personnel decisions and may not be set aside for budget convenience.

### 3.1 Fair Pay, Defined Concretely

**PERS-001** All personnel receive compensation sufficient for dignified life in their context: housing, food, healthcare, education for dependents, retirement provision, recreation, ability to support family.

**PERS-002** "Sufficient" is defined not by local minimum wage (which varies wildly and is often inadequate) but by a documented dignity floor, calibrated by region and family size, reviewed annually.

### 3.2 No Extractive Compensation Structures

**PERS-010** Robotanica is not structured to produce founder, executive, or shareholder windfalls. Compensation includes salary, benefits, and reasonable performance recognition; it does not include equity stakes that produce orders-of-magnitude wealth differentials.

**PERS-011** Where equity-like structures exist (e.g., deferred mission-aligned grants), they are bounded in scale and structured to align personnel with the mission, not to extract value.

### 3.3 Bounded Top-Bottom Ratio

**PERS-020** The ratio between the highest-compensated personnel role and the lowest-compensated full-time role within Robotanica's direct organization may not exceed **8:1**, calculated as total compensation including benefits and any deferred or performance components.

**PERS-021** This ratio is calculated globally across the organization, with regional adjustments by cost-of-living applied to both the numerator and the denominator.

**PERS-022** Federation Operators and partner organizations set their own compensation; the ratio applies to Robotanica's direct organization only. Federation members are encouraged but not required to adopt comparable discipline.

### 3.4 Long-Tenure Rewarded

**PERS-030** Compensation increases with tenure in ways that recognize the institutional knowledge and continuity of long service. Multi-decade careers are an objective; compensation structures support that objective.

### 3.5 Field Work Valued

**PERS-040** Compensation does not systematically privilege office or technical work over field work. A site steward in a remote region performing skilled monitoring work is compensated comparably to an engineer in a capital city performing equivalent-skill technical work, adjusted for cost of living and hardship.

**PERS-041** Hardship and remote-region adjustments are real and material; they are not nominal additions to a base that already disadvantages field workers.

### 3.6 The Long Quiet Years

**PERS-050** Years 5 through 30 of a restoration parcel involve substantial monitoring and tending work that is essential, low-drama, and historically undercompensated. Robotanica explicitly values this work.

**PERS-051** Personnel doing long-horizon work receive compensation that reflects its centrality to the mission, not its excitement value.

### 3.7 Safety as Foundational

**PERS-060** No personnel are asked to work in conditions that endanger their life or health beyond bounds they themselves accept under fully-informed consent.

**PERS-061** Refusal to perform unsafe work is an absolute right; refusal does not affect compensation, standing, or future assignment.

### 3.8 Dissent Protected

**PERS-070** Dissent is welcomed, including dissent from leadership, dissent from majority methodology, dissent from operational decisions. Dissent expressed through legitimate channels is protected from retaliation.

**PERS-071** Whistleblowing — disclosure of conduct violating the Charter, applicable law, or material ethical standards — is structurally protected per §11.

### 3.9 Diversity Built In

**PERS-080** Hiring, promotion, and assignment processes are designed to produce a workforce diverse across geography, gender, culture, methodology, and life experience.

**PERS-081** Diversity is operationalized through specific hiring practices, not through aspirational commitments alone.

### 3.10 Family and Dependents

**PERS-090** Compensation and working conditions support family life. Personnel are not asked to sacrifice family for the mission.

**PERS-091** Where work involves relocation or remote postings, family support is included in the compensation arrangement.

## 4. Compensation Framework

### 4.1 Compensation Components

Total compensation for personnel includes:

- **Base salary** — the primary monetary compensation, calibrated to role and region per §5.
- **Benefits** — health insurance, retirement provision, paid time off, parental leave, dependent education support, and other category-specific benefits.
- **Hardship and field adjustments** — additional compensation for remote postings, dangerous conditions, extreme climates, or other operationally-imposed hardships.
- **Tenure recognition** — increments with years of service per §6.
- **Performance recognition** — modest performance components where appropriate, structured per §7.

### 4.2 Pay Bands

**COMP-101** Compensation is structured in pay bands defined by role category and seniority. Pay bands are published openly within the organization and accessible to personnel.

**COMP-102** Pay bands are reviewed annually and updated to reflect cost-of-living changes, role evolution, and the dignity floor of §3.1.

**COMP-103** Specific compensation within a pay band is determined by skills, tenure, and contribution; differences within bands are bounded to prevent the bands from being notional.

### 4.3 Total Compensation Calculation

**COMP-110** The 8:1 ratio (PERS-020) is calculated on total compensation including all components above, evaluated on consistent terms (e.g., annualized for comparability).

### 4.4 Compensation Reviews

**COMP-120** Personnel compensation is reviewed annually. Reviews consider: tenure progression, role changes, contribution, and external context (cost of living, comparable role compensation in mission-aligned organizations).

**COMP-121** Compensation reductions occur only in defined circumstances: role changes that substantively reduce responsibility, demotion for cause with due process, or organization-wide adjustments tied to documented financial constraints.

## 5. Regional Pay and Parity

The hardest question in any global organization's compensation philosophy is: how does pay differ by location?

### 5.1 The Problem

Two failure modes are common:

- **Globally-uniform pay** in nominal terms means people in low-cost regions are paid effectively at a level vastly above local norms, creating dependence and distorting their relationship to home communities. It also creates effective exclusion: organizations that can only afford to hire at low-region pay end up not hiring anyone from those regions.
- **Local market pay** means people in low-cost regions are paid effectively at a level that may meet local minimum standards but does not provide dignity equivalent to colleagues in higher-cost regions, and creates a structural injustice where the mission is staffed by those who cost the least.

Both fail. Robotanica chooses a third path.

### 5.2 The Dignity Floor

**REG-001** A documented dignity floor is established for each region, calibrated by:

- Cost of housing for a family unit
- Cost of food consistent with regional norms
- Cost of healthcare and education for dependents
- Provision for retirement
- Modest provision for recreation and unexpected expense

This floor is not minimum wage; it is the level at which a household lives with dignity in the region.

**REG-002** The dignity floor is reviewed annually and updated based on documented cost data. It is published.

**REG-003** No personnel may be paid below the regional dignity floor. This is a hard floor.

### 5.3 Above the Floor

**REG-010** Compensation above the dignity floor is calibrated to role and tenure, with regional adjustments that reflect actual cost differences but are bounded.

**REG-011** Specifically: total compensation including the dignity floor never falls below 60% of compensation for the same role in the highest-compensation region, when both are normalized to the dignity floor of their respective regions.

The intent is: a senior engineer in Lagos, in dignity-floor-normalized terms, earns at least 60% of what a senior engineer in San Francisco earns in their dignity-floor-normalized terms. This prevents the structural injustice of paying differently for the same skill while acknowledging real cost differences.

### 5.4 Independence Threshold

**REG-020** Compensation, including hardship adjustments and bonuses, may not so far exceed regional norms that it creates dependence eroding personnel's connection to their home communities or capacity to leave Robotanica without economic catastrophe.

This applies in reverse to the over-compensation problem: in low-cost regions, paying so much above local norms that the personnel become hostage to Robotanica's continued existence is also a form of capture.

### 5.5 Migrating Personnel

**REG-030** Personnel relocating between regions for Robotanica work are supported through transition: relocation expenses, cost-of-living equalization during transition, family support.

**REG-031** Personnel returning home after multi-year remote postings are supported in re-establishment.

### 5.6 Indigenous and Community Personnel

**REG-040** Where personnel are members of indigenous or local communities serving in their own territories, compensation may flow to communities collectively rather than individuals where this is the community's preference under their governance.

**REG-041** Compensation may include in-kind and non-monetary forms (training, infrastructure, knowledge access) where these are valued by the community and consistent with their governance.

**REG-042** Compensation must always provide individuals with the security of being able to leave without their household's livelihood collapsing.

## 6. Tenure Recognition

### 6.1 Why Tenure Matters

Multi-decade work requires multi-decade careers, or at least careers that can develop without continuous compensation pressure to leave for higher pay elsewhere. Tenure recognition is the structural mechanism for this.

### 6.2 Tenure Increments

**TEN-001** Compensation increases with tenure in defined increments at 3, 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, and 30 years of service. Increments reflect:

- Institutional knowledge accumulated.
- Mentorship and capacity-building contributions.
- Reliability and commitment to the long horizon.

### 6.3 Cross-Federation Tenure

**TEN-010** Personnel moving between federation members may carry forward tenure recognition with consent of the receiving member. This supports career continuity within the federation while permitting movement.

### 6.4 Pension and Retirement Provision

**TEN-020** Robotanica provides retirement provision — pension contributions, retirement-fund matching, or comparable structures appropriate to jurisdiction — for all personnel, including field stewards and remote workers.

**TEN-021** Retirement provisions vest progressively and are portable: personnel who leave Robotanica retain the value accrued.

**TEN-022** Where Robotanica's retirement provisions exceed local jurisdictional norms (e.g., in regions with weak public retirement systems), the provisions are calibrated so they support but do not replace public systems where viable.

### 6.5 Sabbaticals

**TEN-030** Personnel with substantial tenure (typically after 7 years) are eligible for sabbatical: paid leave of 3-6 months for rest, learning, or work outside Robotanica.

**TEN-031** Sabbaticals support multi-decade careers by preventing burnout and maintaining personnel's broader connection to the field.

## 7. Performance Recognition

### 7.1 Bounded Performance Components

**PERF-001** Performance components, where they exist, are bounded and structured to reinforce mission-aligned behavior, not to create extreme variance in compensation.

**PERF-002** Performance components do not exceed 15% of base compensation in any role.

### 7.2 What Performance Means

**PERF-010** Performance is evaluated against contributions to mission outcomes, not against proxies that diverge from mission. Specifically:

- For operators: outcomes per MRV (survival rates, biodiversity, soil) over time horizons relevant to the work, not seedlings planted per quarter.
- For engineers: durability, reliability, openness, and mission fit of the systems built, not throughput or velocity in isolation.
- For scientists: contribution to Codex, citation health, peer recognition, traditional knowledge integration.
- For Stewardship Office personnel: relationship health and Lease integrity, not lease count.

**PERF-011** Performance evaluation explicitly avoids metrics that incentivize concealment, optimism bias, or short-termism.

### 7.3 Collective Recognition

**PERF-020** Robotanica favors collective performance recognition (team, region, methodology) over individual performance recognition where possible. This reflects the fundamentally collective nature of restoration.

### 7.4 No Performance-Based Termination Without Process

**PERF-030** Personnel are not terminated for low performance without due process: review, documented expectations, opportunity to improve, and decision by appropriate governance with appeal rights.

## 8. Safety

### 8.1 Physical Safety

**SAFE-001** Personnel are not deployed to operations where their physical safety cannot be reasonably assured.

**SAFE-002** Where field operations involve known hazards (wildlife, weather, terrain, conflict-adjacent contexts), training, equipment, and protocols are provided proportionate to the hazards.

**SAFE-003** Personnel have an absolute right of refusal in unsafe conditions; refusal does not affect compensation or standing.

### 8.2 Conflict and Threat Environments

**SAFE-010** Operations in regions with elevated security risks (armed conflict, political instability, threats to environmental defenders) require:

- Documented risk assessment by qualified parties.
- Personnel consent based on full information about risks.
- Security protocols including evacuation provisions.
- Refusal of operations where risks exceed bounds personnel can reasonably accept.

**SAFE-011** Robotanica refuses operations in environments where personnel cannot operate without unacceptable risk to themselves, regardless of the ecological priority of the region.

### 8.3 Indigenous and Community Personnel Under Threat

**SAFE-020** Indigenous and local community personnel advocating for their communities or for restoration may face threats from external actors (extractive industries, hostile state actors, criminal interests). Robotanica:

- Provides legal support where possible.
- Supports security measures developed by the communities themselves.
- Does not require personnel to remain in unsafe positions; supports relocation or withdrawal as needed.

### 8.4 Psychological Safety

**SAFE-030** Personnel are protected from harassment, abuse, discrimination, and retaliation. Reporting channels are accessible and trusted.

**SAFE-031** Mental health support is included in benefits, particularly for personnel exposed to traumatic events (catastrophic site loss, conflict exposure, sustained difficult conditions).

### 8.5 Family Safety

**SAFE-040** Where personnel work creates risks to family members (relocations, exposure, retaliation against families), family protection is part of safety provision.

## 9. Founders and Senior Leadership

### 9.1 No Founder Windfall

**LEAD-001** Robotanica's founders do not receive equity stakes that produce wealth differentials orders of magnitude beyond personnel.

**LEAD-002** Where founder equity exists, it is bounded and structured to align with mission, not to enable exit-driven extraction.

### 9.2 Leadership Compensation

**LEAD-010** Senior leadership compensation falls within the 8:1 ratio (PERS-020) of the lowest-compensated full-time role.

**LEAD-011** Leadership compensation is published openly per §10.

### 9.3 Leadership Tenure

**LEAD-020** Leadership roles are not for life. Defined terms (typically 5-10 years for executive roles) with renewal subject to governance review prevent indefinite consolidation.

**LEAD-021** Leadership transitions are planned, not crisis events. Succession planning is continuous.

### 9.4 Founder Tenure Limits

**LEAD-030** Founders are subject to the same governance constraints as other leadership. The Charter §8.1 designates founder-related provisions as un-amendable structurally; this Philosophy applies operationally.

### 9.5 Refusal of Capture

**LEAD-040** Leadership compensation may not be increased through funder pressure, partnership terms, or any external influence inconsistent with this Philosophy. Compensation increases pass through the same governance as any other compensation decision.

## 10. Transparency

### 10.1 Pay Bands Public

**TRANS-001** Pay bands and the dignity floor by region are public documents.

### 10.2 Aggregate Compensation Public

**TRANS-010** Aggregate compensation by category and region is published annually: ranges, medians, total payroll. Individual compensation is not public except for senior leadership per §10.3.

### 10.3 Senior Leadership Compensation Public

**TRANS-020** Senior leadership total compensation (named roles within Robotanica's executive structure) is published annually with role identifier.

### 10.4 Personnel Demographics Public

**TRANS-030** Workforce demographics (geographic, gender, role distribution) are published annually in summary form, supporting accountability for the diversity commitments of §3.9.

### 10.5 Confidentiality Boundaries

**TRANS-040** Individual personnel privacy is protected. Specific personal compensation is not disclosed except as required by governance for senior leadership.

## 11. Whistleblower Protection

### 11.1 What Counts

**WB-001** Whistleblowing means disclosure, through defined channels, of conduct that violates the Charter, applicable law, this Philosophy, or material ethical standards.

### 11.2 Channels

**WB-010** Personnel may report concerns through:

- Direct supervisors (where the concern does not implicate them).
- The Stewardship Office (for Charter and structural concerns).
- The Science Council (for methodological and ecological concerns).
- A dedicated whistleblower channel reporting to a governance body distinct from the affected personnel.
- External auditors and regulators where applicable.

### 11.3 Protection From Retaliation

**WB-020** Personnel raising concerns through legitimate channels are protected from retaliation. Retaliation is grounds for discipline including expulsion under the Federation Membership Agreement (§9) and termination under personnel discipline.

**WB-021** Protection extends to: continued employment, compensation, role, references, and reputation within and beyond the Federation.

### 11.4 Anonymity Where Required

**WB-030** Where the whistleblower requires anonymity for safety, anonymity is preserved to the extent consistent with investigation integrity.

### 11.5 Investigation Integrity

**WB-040** Whistleblower complaints are investigated by parties with no conflict of interest in the matter. Complaints involving leadership are investigated by independent investigators.

### 11.6 Reporting on Whistleblowing

**WB-050** Aggregate whistleblower activity (number of reports, categories, outcomes in summary form) is published annually. Specific cases are protected for personnel privacy.

## 12. Refusal of Unethical Orders

### 12.1 Right of Refusal

**REF-001** Personnel have an absolute right to refuse orders or assignments that violate:

- The Charter
- Applicable law
- Material ethical standards
- This Philosophy
- The MRV Specification (for verification-relevant work)
- Indigenous and community sovereignty (per Charter §3.3)

### 12.2 Refusal Without Penalty

**REF-010** Refusal to perform unethical work does not affect compensation, standing, or assignment. Personnel are not required to provide alternative work; they may simply refuse.

### 12.3 Documented Refusals

**REF-020** Refusals are documented in the personnel record (with personnel consent on what is recorded). Pattern of frivolous refusal may be grounds for review; refusal of genuinely unethical work is never grounds for discipline.

### 12.4 Whistleblower Linkage

**REF-030** Refusal coupled with reporting (via §11 channels) is protected as whistleblowing.

## 13. Hiring and Diversity

### 13.1 Hiring Principles

**HIRE-001** Hiring is based on capability, alignment with the mission, and fit for the role.

**HIRE-002** Hiring practices actively counter biases that would otherwise produce homogeneous workforces (geographic concentration, single-gender dominance, single-discipline dominance).

### 13.2 Diversity Targets

**HIRE-010** The Stewardship Office sets and reports against diversity goals across:

- Geographic distribution
- Gender balance
- Cultural and linguistic representation
- Methodological diversity
- Career stage distribution

**HIRE-011** Diversity goals are not quotas applied rigidly; they are measures of progress toward a workforce that reflects the federation's scope.

### 13.3 Capacity Building

**HIRE-020** Where local capacity does not yet exist for roles in a region, Robotanica invests in capacity building (training, education partnerships, internships) rather than importing all personnel from outside the region.

### 13.4 Indigenous and Community Hiring

**HIRE-030** In regions with active indigenous or community partnerships, hiring of community members is prioritized in operational roles (site stewardship, monitoring, fieldwork). Hiring respects community governance over labor allocation.

## 14. Personnel Categories

Different categories of personnel have different employment relationships with Robotanica:

### 14.1 Direct Personnel

Employed directly by Robotanica's organization. Subject in full to this Philosophy and to the Federation Membership Agreement.

### 14.2 Federation Operator Personnel

Employed by Federation Operator organizations. Each Operator sets its own personnel policies; the Federation Membership Agreement obligates Operators to comply with Charter principles, but Operators are not bound to this Philosophy in detail. Operators are encouraged to adopt comparable disciplines.

### 14.3 Contracted Personnel and Consultants

Engaged for specific work products or time-bounded engagements. Compensation is contractually defined; Charter principles apply.

### 14.4 Volunteer Personnel

Where personnel volunteer (typically in civil society participation), compensation provisions do not apply, but safety, dignity, and dissent protections do.

### 14.5 Indigenous and Community Personnel

Engaged through community governance; compensation per §5.6.

## 15. Governance

### 15.1 Personnel Decisions

**GOV-001** Personnel decisions of consequence (hiring senior leadership, terminations, compensation outside bands) are subject to governance review.

**GOV-002** Governance roles in personnel:

- Stewardship Office: oversight of personnel philosophy implementation, dignity floor maintenance, diversity reporting.
- Federation governance body: senior leadership selection, leadership compensation review.
- Independent personnel committee: hiring and termination disputes, whistleblower investigations involving leadership.

### 15.2 Annual Personnel Review

**GOV-010** The Stewardship Office publishes an annual personnel review including:

- Compensation distribution (per §10).
- Diversity metrics (per §13.2).
- Whistleblower activity (per §11.6).
- Identified issues and remediation plans.

### 15.3 Personnel Philosophy Review

**GOV-020** This Philosophy is reviewed every five years comprehensively. Amendments follow Charter and Federation governance processes.

## 16. Open Questions

These are unresolved questions where decisions will have outsized downstream impact:

- **Top-bottom ratio specifics.** The 8:1 ratio is one defensible choice; 5:1 or 12:1 are also defensible. The specific ratio matters less than the commitment to a bounded ratio; the specific value will be debated and may be revised.
- **Dignity floor regional calibration.** Calibrating dignity floors across regions requires regular comparative cost-of-living research that does not currently exist in publishable form for many regions. Robotanica may need to commission this work.
- **Indigenous compensation through community structures.** The community-collective compensation form is recognized in §5.6 but the specific mechanisms (reciprocity, in-kind, infrastructure investment) require partnership-by-partnership design.
- **Federation Operator personnel discipline.** This Philosophy binds Robotanica directly. Federation Operators set their own personnel policies. Whether the Federation Membership Agreement should impose more specific personnel requirements on Operators (and how that interacts with Operator sovereignty) is unresolved.
- **Pension portability across federation.** Multi-decade careers may span multiple Federation members. Cross-Operator pension portability requires structures not yet specified.
- **Performance evaluation of fundamentally collective work.** Performance recognition for individual contributions to fundamentally collective restoration outcomes is hard to do well. The principles are stated; operationalizing them at scale will be ongoing.
- **Catastrophe and personnel.** Climate or political catastrophe may simultaneously affect ecosystems and personnel safety. The order of evacuation, the obligation to communities, the obligation to personnel, can conflict. Specific protocols are not yet developed.
- **AI-assisted work and personnel.** As Robotanica's agents do more work, the personnel role shifts. How this shift is managed (re-skilling, role evolution, ensuring agents do not displace meaningful work) requires explicit design.

## Appendix A — Compensation Component Examples

To illustrate how the compensation components combine:

**A field steward in a tropical pilot region** might receive:
- Base salary at the regional band for skilled field roles
- Hardship adjustment for remote-region posting
- Health insurance covering family
- Pension contribution
- Tenure increment after 5 years
- Annual modest performance recognition
- Sabbatical eligibility after 7 years

Total compensation supports family dignity in the region; the regional dignity floor is exceeded.

**A senior engineer in a high-cost city** might receive:
- Base salary at the regional band for senior engineering roles
- Health insurance covering family
- Retirement contribution
- Tenure increment after 5 years
- Annual modest performance recognition
- Sabbatical eligibility after 7 years

Total compensation, normalized to the regional dignity floor, falls within the 60% band of the highest comparable region (per REG-011) and within the 8:1 ratio of the lowest-compensated full-time role.

**A community-affiliated monitor** in an indigenous-partnership region might receive:
- Compensation flowing partially through community governance structures per community decision
- In-kind support (training, equipment, infrastructure)
- Individual security through guaranteed personal compensation floor
- Recognition of traditional ecological knowledge contribution

## Appendix C — Glossary (Personnel-Specific)

- **Dignity floor** — Region-specific compensation level supporting dignified household life.
- **Federation Operator personnel** — Personnel employed by Federation Operators; not direct Robotanica personnel.
- **Hardship adjustment** — Compensation supplement for remote, dangerous, or extreme conditions.
- **Independence threshold** — The 25% income limit defined in the Science Council Charter §2.5; applied here to prevent capture-creating compensation levels.
- **Long quiet years** — Years 5-30 of restoration parcels involving sustained monitoring and tending.
- **Pay band** — Defined compensation range for role and seniority categories.
- **Pension provision** — Retirement support across personnel categories.
- **Refusal authority** — The absolute right to refuse unsafe or unethical work without penalty.
- **Tenure recognition** — Compensation increments at defined years of service.
- **Top-bottom ratio** — The ratio between highest-compensated and lowest-compensated full-time roles.

*Restoration is done by people. Multi-decade restoration is done by people across decades. The conditions of their work — fair pay, real safety, protected dissent, dignity, family support, opportunity to grow — determine whether restoration continues to be done well or decays into compliance. This Philosophy specifies those conditions because they are not optional. The mission is the work. The work is people. The people are the mission.*

# Public Communication Doctrine

## Specification (v0)

**Document type:** Strategic and operational specification
**Status:** Draft for ratification
**Companion documents:** Robotanica Charter, MRV Specification, Threat Model, Funding Strategy, Federation Membership Agreement

---

## 1. Purpose and Status

This document specifies how Robotanica communicates publicly. It defines:

- The principles governing all public communication.
- The categories of communication and how each is governed.
- The treatment of failure, crisis, and unflattering information.
- The treatment of success, marketing, and fundraising.
- The role of personnel speaking publicly in personal and official capacities.
- How indigenous and community voice is respected.
- How critique is engaged.

Charter §2.9 commits Robotanica to transparent failure. Threat Model §4.1 names transparency drift as a mission drift vector. Threat Model §6.5 names the catastrophic site scenario where a flagship restoration site fails publicly. None of those documents specifies what transparent failure looks like in practice — what goes in the press release, who decides, what is automatic, what is restricted.

This Doctrine specifies it. Its primary discipline is restraint: communicating only what the evidence supports, no more; communicating failures with the same prominence as successes; refusing to use Robotanica's communication channels for purposes incompatible with the mission.

A note on tone. Most communication doctrines from large organizations are defensive PR manuals. This document is something else. It is a commitment, in operational form, to communicate the way the Charter promises Robotanica will: honestly, plainly, with attention to power and to those whose stories are not ours to tell.

---

## 2. Why This Matters

Restoration is a public domain. The work happens on land owned by others, in ecosystems shared with all, with consequences flowing to people not yet born. Public communication is therefore not optional marketing; it is part of the work.

It is also adversarial terrain. The Threat Model identifies several communication-adjacent risks:

- **Capture by greenwashing partnerships** — Robotanica's communication channels could be used to legitimize partners' broader practices.
- **Mission drift through metric drift** — favorable metrics replacing true ones in public claims.
- **Transparency drift** — failure reporting becoming softer, qualified, eventually omitted.
- **Catastrophic site events** — flagship sites failing in ways that require honest communication during media pressure.
- **Bad-faith critique** — campaigns to undermine the project that require disciplined response.

The discipline of this Doctrine is the structural defense against these risks.

## 3. Principles

These principles bind public communication and may not be set aside for messaging convenience.

### 3.1 Honesty Always

**COMM-001** Public communication is honest. Robotanica does not communicate things it knows to be false, materially incomplete in ways that mislead, or more certain than the evidence supports.

**COMM-002** Where uncertainty is real, it is communicated. Ranges, confidence intervals, methodological caveats, and limitations are stated, not concealed.

### 3.2 Reporting Symmetry

**COMM-010** Failures are communicated on the same cadence and with the same prominence as successes (per MRV §6.5). A failed cohort is reported with no less detail than a successful one. A reconciled credit is no less visible than the original claim.

### 3.3 Restraint in Claims

**COMM-020** Robotanica claims credit for outcomes that the evidence supports, attributable to its work per MRV attribution discipline (§9). Robotanica does not claim outcomes that would have occurred regardless of its work, nor outcomes from work performed by others.

**COMM-021** Where outcomes are partial, Robotanica says so. Where outcomes are uncertain, Robotanica says so. Where outcomes are projected rather than verified, Robotanica says so explicitly.

### 3.4 Voice Discipline

**COMM-030** Robotanica does not speak for those whose voices are their own to use. Specifically:

- Indigenous communities speak for themselves about their lands, knowledge, and decisions.
- Landowners speak for themselves about their parcels and arrangements.
- Federation members speak for their own work in their own voice.
- Personnel speak for themselves in personal capacity (per §12).

**COMM-031** Robotanica's institutional voice is reserved for matters genuinely institutional: protocol, governance, aggregate outcomes, policy positions, decisions of consequence.

### 3.5 No Channel Capture

**COMM-040** Robotanica's communication channels are not used to legitimize practices incompatible with the Charter, including those of partners or funders. Co-branding, partnership announcements, and joint communications are permitted only where the partner's work is itself compatible with Charter principles.

**COMM-041** Robotanica's channels are not used to advertise products, attack competitors, or perform political endorsements outside the project's scope.

### 3.6 Plain Language

**COMM-050** Public communication is plain. Technical detail is available where relevant; jargon and obscurity are avoided.

**COMM-051** Communication respects the reader: assumes intelligence, does not condescend, makes the substance accessible without dumbing it down.

### 3.7 Provenance for Claims

**COMM-060** Specific outcome claims in public communication reference the underlying records — observations in Gaia, verification reports, governance decisions — so claims can be traced and audited.

### 3.8 Refusal of Polish Over Truth

**COMM-070** Communication is not polished into untruth. Where the polished version is misleading and the unpolished version is accurate, the unpolished version is published.

**COMM-071** Where producing accurate communication takes time, communication is delayed rather than rushed into approximation. "We are still gathering information" is preferred to "Here is our best guess presented as fact."

### 3.9 Respect Across Languages

**COMM-080** Public communication is published in the languages of the audiences it concerns. Communications affecting indigenous and local communities are published in the languages of those communities.

### 3.10 Privacy Honored

**COMM-090** Communication respects the privacy of personnel, partners, and individuals associated with the work. PII is not disclosed except where required by governance or law.

## 4. Categories of Public Communication

Robotanica's public communication breaks into categories with distinct disciplines.

### 4.1 The Public Record (Automatic)

The public record per the platform PRDs and Protocol Specifications: governance decisions, Lease lifecycle events, recipe revisions, credit issuance and reconciliation, federation membership changes, MRV verification reports, agent action logs in summary form, Council decisions, dispute outcomes.

The public record is **automatic and obligatory**. It is published by the platforms continuously. It is not edited for messaging purposes. It is the default form of transparency.

### 4.2 Routine Reporting

Operational reports, outcome reports, annual reports, audit reports, decadal reviews. These are scheduled communications that aggregate and contextualize the public record for accessible consumption.

Routine reporting is **scheduled and disciplined**. It uses the metrics specified in MRV (§6) and the formats required by the Charter and audit requirements.

### 4.3 Press and Media Engagement

Communication with journalists, broadcasters, and media-platform-driven outlets. Includes responses to inquiries, proactive announcements, interviews, and opinion pieces.

Press engagement is **active but disciplined**. Robotanica does not avoid press; it engages on its terms with the principles of §3.

### 4.4 Crisis Communication

Communication during acute events: site catastrophe, public dispute, media frenzy, regulatory action, security incident.

Crisis communication is **honest and timely**. It is not damage control; it is fact reporting under pressure.

### 4.5 Failure Communication

Communication of restoration failure, methodological error, project setback, or institutional mistake.

Failure communication is **deliberate and prominent** per §3.2 reporting symmetry.

### 4.6 Fundraising and Mission Communication

Communication intended to attract support, funding, federation members, or new partners.

Fundraising communication is **honest about uncertainty**. It does not represent the project as more certain, more advanced, or more successful than it is. It refuses claims that would mislead potential supporters.

### 4.7 Stakeholder Communication

Direct communication with Landowners, communities, federation members, funders, and partners. Often private rather than fully public.

Stakeholder communication is **respectful and consistent**. The same standards of honesty apply; what is said in a stakeholder context must not contradict what is said in public.

### 4.8 Personnel-Capacity Communication

Public statements by personnel speaking in personal capacity: academic publications, conference talks, social media, op-eds, podcasts.

Personnel communication is **enabled and protected** per §12.

## 5. The Public Record

### 5.1 Source of Truth

**REC-001** The public record is the canonical source of factual claims about Robotanica. Other communication forms reference and contextualize the public record; they do not contradict it.

### 5.2 Coverage

**REC-010** The public record covers (per Protocol §10.4 and individual platform PRDs):

- Governance decisions and votes
- Lease lifecycle events (formation, amendment, succession, termination, closeout)
- Recipe revisions
- Credit issuance, transfer, retirement, reconciliation
- Federation membership changes
- MRV verification reports
- Council decisions and Council member disclosures
- Funding relationships above materiality threshold
- Aggregate workforce metrics

### 5.3 Coarsening for Sovereignty and Privacy

**REC-020** Public-record content honors sovereignty per Protocol §10 and Charter §3.3. Sensitive locations are coarsened. Indigenous and community data is published only as the sovereign party permits.

**REC-021** PII is omitted or pseudonymized except where governance roles require disclosure (e.g., senior leadership compensation per Personnel Philosophy §10.3).

### 5.4 Stability

**REC-030** Public-record entries are immutable per Protocol §3.1. Corrections are made through superseding entries that reference the prior state, not through silent edits.

### 5.5 Accessibility

**REC-040** The public record is published in machine-readable open formats (per the Data Lifecycle and Preservation Plan) and in human-readable summaries.

**REC-041** Search, filtering, and standard query interfaces are provided.

**REC-042** Aggregations and reports drawn from the public record are published in the languages of the audiences served.

## 6. Routine Reporting

### 6.1 Annual Report

**ROUT-001** Robotanica publishes an annual report covering:

- Aggregate restoration outcomes (per MRV)
- Lease portfolio (count, area, regions, term distribution)
- Operational scale (cohorts, biological production, fleet size, operations performed)
- Federation status (member counts, regional distribution)
- Governance activity (decisions, vetoes, disputes)
- Funding mix (sources, concentration, refusals)
- Workforce demographics
- Identified risks and mitigation status
- Open issues and forward priorities

**ROUT-002** The annual report is honest about partial outcomes, uncertain claims, and ongoing failures. It does not present a curated success narrative.

### 6.2 Decadal Review

**ROUT-010** Every ten years, Robotanica conducts a decadal review covering longer-horizon trajectories, multi-decade outcomes, and structural assessment of the project.

**ROUT-011** The decadal review includes external perspectives — peer reviewers, independent assessors, community voices — alongside internal assessment.

### 6.3 Sub-Annual Reporting

**ROUT-020** Quarterly or semi-annual reporting covers operational progress, federation activity, and notable events. Less comprehensive than annual but maintains continuous public visibility.

### 6.4 Reporting on Open Topics

**ROUT-030** Topics designated as "open questions" in Robotanica's documents (Charter, PRDs, this Doctrine) are reported on as decisions are made or progress occurs. Open questions do not languish without periodic update.

### 6.5 Reporting Cadence Discipline

**ROUT-040** Reporting cadence is maintained even during difficult periods. A year in which performance is poor produces a report; a year of leadership transition produces a report; a year of crisis produces a report.

## 7. Press and Media Engagement

### 7.1 Engagement Posture

**PRESS-001** Robotanica engages with press actively. Avoidance is not the discipline; honesty is.

**PRESS-002** Engagement is on Robotanica's terms regarding accuracy and context, not regarding favorability. A journalist who reports unfavorable but accurate findings receives the same engagement as one who reports favorable findings.

### 7.2 Spokespersons

**PRESS-010** Designated spokespersons speak for Robotanica institutionally:

- The CEO or executive director speaks on overall direction, major decisions, and crisis events.
- Subject-matter leads speak on their domains (Stewardship Office on Leases and communities; Council Chair on scientific matters; CFO on funding).
- Regional spokespersons speak on regional matters, often in regional languages.

**PRESS-011** Spokesperson roles are public knowledge; press knows whom to contact for what.

### 7.3 Inquiry Response

**PRESS-020** Inquiries from press receive timely response. Refusal to respond is itself a response and is disclosed (e.g., "Robotanica declined to comment on personnel matter pending investigation").

**PRESS-021** Robotanica does not refuse to respond to negative inquiries while accepting positive ones. Symmetry of engagement is maintained.

### 7.4 Press Releases and Proactive Announcements

**PRESS-030** Press releases are issued for matters of genuine public interest: major decisions, significant federation events, crisis communication, milestone reports.

**PRESS-031** Press releases reflect §3 principles: no over-claiming, no greenwashing, no hidden caveats.

### 7.5 Embargoed and Off-the-Record

**PRESS-040** Embargoes and off-the-record discussions are used sparingly and consistently. Embargoes are honored when accepted; off-the-record agreements are honored absolutely.

### 7.6 Hostile Press

**PRESS-050** Where press coverage is hostile, Robotanica responds factually: corrections of factual errors, context where context is missing, refusal to engage in personalized attack.

**PRESS-051** Hostile press is not retaliated against through access denial, legal threat (except where genuine defamation occurs), or coordinated push-back campaigns.

## 8. Crisis Communication

### 8.1 What Constitutes Crisis

**CRIS-001** Crisis communication situations include:

- Catastrophic site events (fire, severe disease, mass mortality)
- Public allegations of misconduct (Operator fraud, indigenous rights violation, etc.)
- Major dispute with high external visibility
- Regulatory action against Robotanica or major federation member
- Cyber attack or data breach
- Personnel safety incident
- Major reconciliation event affecting many credits
- External catastrophe affecting Federation operations (war, disaster)

### 8.2 Response Speed

**CRIS-010** Initial response is timely (within 24 hours for major crises) acknowledging awareness and stating that a fuller response will follow.

**CRIS-011** Robotanica refuses to "wait until we know everything" before acknowledging events. Initial honest "we don't yet know X but we know Y" is preferred to delayed perfect statements.

### 8.3 Response Substance

**CRIS-020** Crisis communication includes:

- What is known
- What is not yet known
- What is being done
- Who is leading the response
- When further information will be available

**CRIS-021** Crisis communication does not include:

- Speculation that may prove wrong
- Defensive reframing of the underlying event
- Attribution of blame to parties not yet established as responsible
- Promotional content unrelated to the crisis

### 8.4 Crisis Authority

**CRIS-030** During crisis, designated crisis-response leadership has authority to make timely public statements within established bounds. This is not a license to override governance; it is operational authority within pre-approved parameters.

**CRIS-031** Decisions of larger consequence — Lease termination, recipe revision, governance restructuring — follow normal governance processes even during crisis.

### 8.5 Crisis Documentation

**CRIS-040** Every crisis produces a post-event documentation: timeline, decisions made, communications issued, outcomes, lessons. This is published as part of the public record (subject to privacy and ongoing-investigation constraints).

### 8.6 Long-Tail Communication

**CRIS-050** Crises do not end with the news cycle. Ongoing communication about resolution, reconciliation, and lessons continues for as long as the matter remains active, even when external attention has moved on.

## 9. Failure Communication

### 9.1 Reporting Symmetry Operationalized

**FAIL-001** Failures are communicated through the same channels, on the same cadence, and with the same prominence as successes. A flagship failure is treated as comparable in visibility to the flagship success it replaced.

### 9.2 Categories of Failure Communication

Failures fall into types, each with appropriate communication form:

- **Site-level failure** — single parcel or cohort failure. Reported in routine outcome reports; significant failures (flagship sites, public-interest sites) trigger specific communication.
- **Methodology failure** — recipe found inadequate or wrong. Reported through Codex revision communication and methodology updates.
- **Verification failure** — falsification or material error in verification. Reported with full transparency including parties involved (subject to ongoing-investigation constraints).
- **Operator failure** — Operator misconduct or incompetence. Reported through Federation membership communication and dispute outcomes.
- **Institutional failure** — Robotanica's own error. Reported by leadership directly, without attribution-shifting.

### 9.3 Failure Narrative Discipline

**FAIL-010** Failure communication does not seek silver linings or inverted positive framings. A failure is a failure. Lessons may be drawn, but lessons do not soften the report of what happened.

**FAIL-011** Communication does not use failures of others (climate, weather, third parties) to deflect from Robotanica's own contributions to outcomes.

### 9.4 Reconciliation Communication

**FAIL-020** Credit reconciliation events (per MRV §14, A3 §6.6) are communicated specifically: which credits are voided or made whole, on what evidence, with what effect on holders and counterparties.

**FAIL-021** Reconciliation communication is no less prominent than the original credit issuance communication.

### 9.5 Personnel Accountability

**FAIL-030** Failure communication does not blame personnel for systemic failures. It also does not protect personnel responsible for individual misconduct from appropriate accountability through Personnel Philosophy and Federation Membership Agreement processes.

### 9.6 Cooperation With Investigators

**FAIL-040** When external investigators (regulators, journalists, civil society) examine Robotanica failures, Robotanica cooperates within the bounds of legal advice and personnel privacy, not within the bounds of public-relations preference.

## 10. Fundraising and Mission Communication

### 10.1 Honest About Uncertainty

**FUND-001** Fundraising communication does not represent the project as more certain, more advanced, or more successful than it is.

**FUND-002** Specifically: pre-issued credits cannot be marketed as if issued. Pilot results cannot be extrapolated to project-wide claims. Demonstrated outcomes are not blended with hoped-for outcomes.

### 10.2 Refusal to Use Outcomes Inappropriately

**FUND-010** Outcomes from indigenous-partnered work are not used in fundraising communication without community consent, even where such use is technically permitted.

**FUND-011** Specific Lease relationships are not foregrounded in fundraising without Landowner consent.

### 10.3 Funder Recognition Discipline

**FUND-020** Funder recognition in Robotanica's communication is proportionate, factual, and does not imply endorsement of funders' broader practices (per Funding Strategy §3.3).

### 10.4 No Outcome Inflation

**FUND-030** Fundraising materials do not inflate outcomes (numbers, areas, claims). Where headline numbers are used, they are sourced from the verified public record and are accompanied by methodological context.

### 10.5 Photography and Imagery Discipline

**FUND-040** Imagery used in fundraising is sourced honestly: actual Robotanica work, not staged scenes, not images of others' work used as if Robotanica's, not pre-Robotanica images presented as post-restoration.

**FUND-041** Imagery involving people is consent-based: those depicted have agreed to the use, in the form used, for the purposes used.

### 10.6 Mission Communication Beyond Fundraising

**FUND-050** Communication intended to advance the mission broadly (advocacy, education, public discourse) holds the same disciplines: honest, restrained, attribution-aware, sovereignty-respecting.

## 11. Stakeholder Communication

### 11.1 Landowner Communication

**STAKE-001** Landowners receive communication per their Lease terms, with the same accuracy and completeness applied to public communication.

**STAKE-002** Communication to Landowners about their parcels includes outcomes, deviations, risks, and decisions affecting them, in their language.

### 11.2 Indigenous and Community Communication

**STAKE-010** Communities receive communication on terms set by their governance structures, often distinct from Robotanica's general communication processes.

**STAKE-011** Robotanica does not communicate publicly about indigenous-partnered work in ways the community has not approved, even where the underlying information is technically not subject to sovereignty constraints.

### 11.3 Federation Member Communication

**STAKE-020** Federation members receive operational and governance communication relevant to their participation, including: protocol changes, governance decisions, marketplace events, performance feedback.

**STAKE-021** Internal federation communication and public communication are consistent. What is said internally is not contradicted publicly.

### 11.4 Funder Communication

**STAKE-030** Funders receive use-of-funds reporting (per Funding Strategy §9.2) at granularity proportionate to their funding scope.

**STAKE-031** Funders receive the same accuracy and completeness as public reporting; favorable accounting for funders is refused.

### 11.5 Government and Regulator Communication

**STAKE-040** Communication with regulators, governments, and multilateral bodies follows applicable legal requirements and the principles of this Doctrine. Cooperation with legitimate regulatory inquiry is provided.

## 12. Personnel Speaking

### 12.1 Right to Speak

**PERS-001** Personnel have the right to speak publicly in personal capacity about their work, the field, and matters of public interest.

**PERS-002** Personnel exercising this right are protected per the Charter's commitment to dissent (§3.5) and the Personnel Philosophy (§3.8).

### 12.2 Personal Capacity vs. Institutional Voice

**PERS-010** Personnel speaking in personal capacity make this clear; their statements are theirs, not Robotanica's institutional voice.

**PERS-011** Personnel do not represent themselves as institutional spokespersons unless they are designated as such for specific topics.

### 12.3 Confidentiality and Sovereignty

**PERS-020** Personnel honor confidentiality and sovereignty constraints in personal-capacity speaking: indigenous data sovereignty, sensitive locations, PII, ongoing-investigation matters.

**PERS-021** Personnel disclose Robotanica affiliations when speaking on Robotanica-adjacent matters.

### 12.4 Critique of Robotanica

**PERS-030** Personnel may publicly critique Robotanica's decisions, methodology, or direction, in personal capacity, without institutional retaliation.

**PERS-031** Critique should be substantive, factually grounded, and engage the merits. Mere disparagement is not protected; reasoned dissent is.

### 12.5 Personnel as Subject-Matter Voices

**PERS-040** Personnel are encouraged to participate in scientific publications, conferences, and public discourse on the substance of their work. This advances the field and supports the openness commitments of the Charter.

### 12.6 Crisis Communication and Personnel

**PERS-050** During crises, personnel may need to refrain from public statements to allow coordinated institutional response. This restraint is bounded in time and scope; it does not substitute for personnel's ongoing right to speak.

## 13. Indigenous and Community Voice

### 13.1 Sovereignty Over Communication

**IND-001** Indigenous and local communities have sovereignty over communication concerning their lands, knowledge, and decisions per Charter §3.3 and Protocol §10.

**IND-002** Robotanica does not produce communication featuring or representing indigenous or community work without the relevant community's consent on the specific communication.

### 13.2 Centering Community Voice

**IND-010** Where communication concerns indigenous-partnered work, the community's own voice is centered. Robotanica's institutional voice supports, contextualizes, and amplifies — it does not replace.

### 13.3 Community Communication Through Robotanica Channels

**IND-020** Communities may use Robotanica's communication channels for their own communication on their terms, when they choose to. This is partnership, not substitution.

### 13.4 Misrepresentation and Correction

**IND-030** Where Robotanica's communication is found to misrepresent indigenous or community work, correction is prompt and prominent, not buried.

### 13.5 Translation and Linguistic Access

**IND-040** Communications affecting indigenous and local communities are produced in their languages, by speakers of those languages where possible, with translation provenance per the CARE Principles.

## 14. Critique Response

### 14.1 Critique Welcomed

**CRIT-001** External critique is welcomed as a discipline. It strengthens the work; it surfaces blind spots; it tests the commitments.

### 14.2 Engagement With Substantive Critique

**CRIT-010** Substantive critique — engaging methodology, evidence, decisions, principles — is engaged substantively. Robotanica responds with reasoning, evidence, and acknowledgment of where critique has merit.

**CRIT-011** Where substantive critique reveals genuine error, error is corrected and acknowledged.

### 14.3 Distinction From Bad-Faith Attack

**CRIT-020** Bad-faith attacks — coordinated campaigns to undermine the project, not to improve it — are distinguished from substantive critique through patterns: lack of evidence, mischaracterization, ad hominem, repetition of refuted claims.

**CRIT-021** Bad-faith attacks receive limited engagement: factual corrections where appropriate; refusal to amplify; refusal to use Robotanica's own standing to legitimize the attack through over-engagement.

### 14.4 Difficult Critique From Within

**CRIT-030** Critique from federation members, communities, or personnel is engaged through governance processes appropriate to the source: dispute resolution, governance review, formal consultation. It is not redirected to public communication channels to be defused.

### 14.5 Public Acknowledgment of Critique

**CRIT-040** Robotanica's public reporting includes acknowledgment of significant external critique, summarized and engaged, rather than ignoring critique that exists.

## 15. Authority and Governance

### 15.1 Communication Governance

**GOV-001** Public communication is governed by:

- The Stewardship Office for Lease, community, and long-horizon communication.
- The Federation governance body for federation-wide and policy communication.
- The Science Council for scientific and methodological communication within Council scope.
- Designated communication leadership (Communications Director or equivalent) for day-to-day execution.

### 15.2 Communication Office

**GOV-010** A Communications Office within Robotanica's organization handles:

- Press engagement
- Public record curation and accessibility
- Routine reporting production
- Crisis response coordination
- Translation and linguistic accessibility
- Coordination across regions and federation members

The Communications Office serves the principles and disciplines of this Doctrine; it does not own the institutional voice.

### 15.3 Spokesperson Designation

**GOV-020** Spokespersons are designated by the Communications Office in coordination with relevant governance bodies. Designations are public.

### 15.4 Review of This Doctrine

**GOV-030** This Doctrine is reviewed every five years comprehensively. Amendments follow Charter and Federation governance processes.

### 15.5 Disputes Within Communication

**GOV-040** Disputes regarding communication (allegations of inaccuracy, complaints from stakeholders, internal disagreement on messaging) are resolved through governance: Communications Office, escalating to relevant governance bodies.

## 16. Open Questions

- **Social media discipline.** Social media has rapid cycles and adversarial dynamics that this Doctrine partially addresses. Specific protocols for social media engagement (when to engage, when to refuse, who speaks) need more concrete development.
- **AI-generated communication.** As Robotanica's agents draft routine communication, the line between agent-drafted-and-human-reviewed and agent-published needs explicit specification. The current Doctrine assumes human authorship; emerging practice will require updates.
- **Brand and visual identity.** Communication has a visual dimension this Doctrine does not detail. Logo, colors, design language need separate specification, with the same restraint principles applied.
- **Attribution conventions for federation work.** When work is performed by Federation members under Robotanica protocols, whose voice tells the story is contested. The Doctrine sketches principles; specific conventions need development.
- **Research publication relationships.** Scientific Contributors publishing about Codex work involves complex authorship, citation, and credit dynamics. Specific guidance for academic publication relationships is needed.
- **Engagement with hostile state communication.** Some governments may produce communication directly hostile to Robotanica or Federation members. Engagement with state media, state-aligned outlets, and state campaigns requires specific protocols.
- **Long-horizon archives of communication.** Public communication itself is preserved per the Data Lifecycle and Preservation Plan. The standards for what is preserved (every press release, every social media post, only formal documents) need clarification.
- **Crisis surge capacity.** The Communications Office must absorb crisis-time surge in volume and complexity. Surge capacity (additional personnel, external partnerships, mutual aid arrangements) requires explicit planning.

## Appendix A — Common Failure Modes Avoided

This Doctrine is designed to avoid common failure modes of mission-driven organization communication:

- **The annual report that hides bad years.** Avoided by reporting symmetry (§3.2) and routine reporting discipline (§6.5).
- **The crisis that becomes a slow-motion cover-up.** Avoided by §8 crisis communication discipline.
- **The impact claim that didn't really happen.** Avoided by §3.3 restraint and §10.4 no outcome inflation.
- **The greenwashing partnership announcement.** Avoided by §3.5 no channel capture and §10.3 funder recognition discipline.
- **The community story used without permission.** Avoided by §13 indigenous and community voice.
- **The hostile press that gets stonewalled.** Avoided by §7.6 hostile press response.
- **The personnel critic who gets fired.** Avoided by §12.4 critique of Robotanica protections.
- **The polished claim that buries the truth.** Avoided by §3.8 refusal of polish over truth.

## Appendix C — Glossary (Communication-Specific)

- **Crisis communication** — Communication during acute events requiring timely public response.
- **Failure communication** — Communication of restoration failure, methodological error, or institutional mistake.
- **Institutional voice** — Robotanica's voice as an organization, distinct from personnel personal capacity.
- **Personal capacity** — Speech by personnel as themselves, not as Robotanica spokespersons.
- **Public record** — The automatic, immutable record of decisions and events per Protocol §10.4.
- **Reporting symmetry** — The principle that failures are reported with the same prominence as successes.
- **Restraint in claims** — The principle that Robotanica claims only what evidence supports.
- **Spokesperson** — Designated personnel authorized to speak in institutional voice.
- **Stakeholder communication** — Direct communication with Landowners, communities, federation members, and funders.

---

*Communication is not the work; communication is part of the work. The way Robotanica speaks publicly determines whether the trust the work depends on can be maintained, whether the mistakes can be acknowledged and learned from, and whether the voices of those whose lands and stories carry the work are honored. This Doctrine is the discipline that makes communication an extension of the mission, not its compromise.*

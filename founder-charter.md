# Founder Charter

## Template for Future Founders (v0)

**Document type:** Founder commitment template
**Status:** Template draft. To be adapted and signed by actual founders when identified.
**Companion documents:** Robotanica Charter, Funding Strategy, Founding Capital Plan, Personnel and Compensation Philosophy, Threat Model

---

## 1. Purpose and Status

This document is a **template** for the specific commitments founders make to Robotanica.

The corpus refers to "founders" repeatedly — as the parties who initially commit to the project, who hold initial capital, who establish governance, and who ratify the Charter. The Threat Model identifies founders as an adversary class (§3.7); the Charter §8 specifies un-amendable provisions limiting founder authority; the Personnel and Compensation Philosophy §9 specifies no founder windfalls; the Funding Strategy and Founding Capital Plan reference founder commitments without specifying their content.

But no specific founders have been named. Writing a "Founder Charter" with specific terms would be premature.

This document is therefore a **template**: a framework that specifies what founders, when identified, will commit to. Real founders will adapt this template, possibly modify specific provisions consistent with the underlying Charter, and sign it as their explicit commitment to the project.

The document is **substantive even as template**. It articulates what founder commitment means within Robotanica — what founders bring, what founders accept, what founders refuse, and what protections exist against founder capture. Future founders will recognize themselves in it or recognize the misalignment and decline.

## 2. The Founder Question

### 2.1 Why Founders Need Specific Commitments

Founder roles are unique in any organization:

- Founders disproportionately shape early decisions that have long-term consequences.
- Founders often hold substantial initial influence (relationships, knowledge, capital).
- Founders have outsized symbolic weight; the project is associated with them publicly.
- Founder departure can disrupt the organization significantly.

The Charter recognizes these dynamics through structural defenses (un-amendable provisions, governance constraints, succession requirements). But beyond structural defenses, founders make personal commitments that shape how the structural defenses actually operate.

### 2.2 The Founder-Capture Problem

The Threat Model §3.7 names founders as an adversary class — not because founders intend harm, but because founder positions create capture risks:

- Founders may resist transferring authority to governance bodies.
- Founders may resist amending decisions made by them in the founding period.
- Founders may attract patron-style capital that creates dependence.
- Founders may take public positions as if speaking for the federation.
- Founders may benefit financially from the project's success in ways that compromise the no-windfall principle.

These risks exist not because founders are bad but because founder positions concentrate authority in ways the federation needs to resist over time. The Founder Charter is the explicit structural and personal commitment to that resistance.

### 2.3 What Founders Bring

Founders bring substantial things to the project:

- Initial vision and conviction.
- Relationships and networks.
- Domain expertise.
- Initial capital (personal or attracted).
- Time and energy at high intensity in the founding period.
- Personal reputation lent to the project.

These contributions are valuable. They warrant recognition. The recognition is bounded by the Charter and this Founder Charter; it is not unlimited and it does not produce extractive equity structures.

### 2.4 The Founder-Steward Distinction

Founders are not the same as Stewards. The Charter §3 articulates a Steward's Covenant binding everyone in Robotanica's operations. Founders are subject to the Steward's Covenant and may continue as Stewards after their founder period.

The founder period is bounded — typically 3-7 years from project formation. After that period, founders transition to other roles (Stewards, advisors, retired).

## 3. Founder Commitments

These are the commitments founders make. The list applies to all founders; specific founders may add personal commitments consistent with this template.

### 3.1 Charter Primacy

**FND-001** Founders commit to the Charter as the supreme document of Robotanica. Founder authority is bounded by the Charter; un-amendable provisions (Charter §8.1) are not subject to founder revision.

**FND-002** Where founder preferences conflict with the Charter, the Charter governs.

### 3.2 Bounded Authority

**FND-010** Founders accept bounded authority:

- Founder decisions in the founding period (typically Phase 0 and Phase 1 per Founding Capital Plan) are subject to ongoing governance establishment.
- Founder authority transfers to constituted governance bodies as those bodies establish.
- Founders do not retain veto authority over governance decisions beyond the bounds the Charter specifies.

### 3.3 No Windfall

**FND-020** Founders accept the no-windfall principle:

- No equity stakes producing wealth differentials orders of magnitude beyond personnel.
- No deferred compensation that produces founder windfalls at exit or maturity.
- Compensation per Personnel Philosophy §3.3 (8:1 top-bottom ratio) applies to founders as to any senior leadership.

**FND-021** Founder capital contributions (personal investment, sweat equity during pre-revenue period) are recognized as part of total founder contribution but do not become claims on disproportionate value.

### 3.4 Time-Bounded Founder Period

**FND-030** Founders accept that the founder period is time-bounded:

- The founding period extends approximately 3-7 years from project formation, depending on the founder's role.
- Founder period ends when:
  - Replacement leadership for the founder's role is in place.
  - Founder voluntarily transitions out.
  - Five years have passed and the federation governance approves a transition timeline.
- After the founder period, the former founder may continue in Robotanica in other roles (Steward, advisor, etc.) or depart.

### 3.5 Public Voice Discipline

**FND-040** Founders accept discipline on public voice:

- During the founder period, founders may speak publicly on behalf of Robotanica per Public Communication Doctrine §7.
- Founders' public statements bind Robotanica only when made within their authorized role.
- Founders' personal-capacity statements (per Public Communication Doctrine §12) are clearly distinguished.
- After the founder period, former founders speak in personal capacity unless specifically re-authorized.

### 3.6 Funder Relationship Discipline

**FND-050** Founders accept discipline on funder relationships:

- Founders attract and engage funders per Funding Strategy and Founding Capital Plan.
- Founders cannot bind Robotanica to funder terms incompatible with the Charter or the Funding Strategy.
- Founders refuse funding categorically per Funding Strategy §5, even if personally relationships exist with refused funders.
- Founders' personal relationships with funders are disclosed to the Funding Committee.

### 3.7 Governance Building, Not Holding

**FND-060** Founders commit to building governance bodies, not holding governance authority:

- Stewardship Office stood up early in the founding period.
- Science Council seated as soon as feasible (per Council Charter requirements).
- Federation governance established with member participation as members onboard.
- AI Ethics Review Board established before substantial agent deployment.

**FND-061** Founders do not occupy governance roles permanently. Where founders hold initial governance positions (e.g., founding board), term limits and succession apply.

### 3.8 Threat Model Acknowledgment

**FND-070** Founders explicitly acknowledge the Threat Model's identification of founders as an adversary class:

- Acceptance that the structural defenses against founder capture apply to them personally.
- Welcome of the structural defenses rather than resentment.
- Constructive engagement with succession protocols.
- Acknowledgment that founder departure (voluntary or otherwise) is not failure but normal organizational evolution.

### 3.9 Personnel Treatment Discipline

**FND-080** Founders commit to treating personnel per the Personnel and Compensation Philosophy:

- Personnel are not asked to sacrifice in founder service.
- Founder convenience does not override personnel rights.
- Founder relationships with personnel are professional, not patron-client.

### 3.10 Indigenous and Community Sovereignty

**FND-090** Founders commit to Charter §3.3 indigenous and community sovereignty:

- Founders do not negotiate or compromise community sovereignty for project advantage.
- Founder relationships with communities are partnerships, not patronage.
- Founders defer to community governance on matters within community scope.

### 3.11 Mission Conscience Beyond Founder Role

**FND-100** Founders accept that the mission outlives the founder role:

- Mission decisions made during founding may be revised by subsequent governance.
- Founder contributions become institutional history, not founder property.
- Founder satisfaction with the project is not a constraint on the project's evolution.

## 4. Founder Recognition

Founders contribute substantially. The Charter and this Founder Charter recognize this within bounds.

### 4.1 Recognition Forms

**REC-001** Founder recognition includes:

- Public acknowledgment as founders in the public record.
- Documented contribution in the project's institutional history.
- Continued engagement in advisory or alumni capacities if mutual interest exists.
- Compensation during the founder period at appropriate senior levels (per Personnel Philosophy §3.3 ratio).
- Possible deferred compensation aligned with sustainability per Personnel Philosophy §9.

### 4.2 What Recognition Does Not Include

**REC-010** Founder recognition does not include:

- Equity windfalls.
- Permanent governance positions.
- Veto authority over mission direction.
- Co-ownership of intellectual property (per Charter §5.3).
- Personal control of brand or reputation beyond the founder period.

### 4.3 Founder Capital Treatment

**REC-020** Founder personal capital invested in the project:

- Is recognized as documented contribution.
- May be returned per agreed terms during the founder period or after.
- Does not produce equity stakes in the conventional sense.
- Is bounded by the no-windfall principle.

### 4.4 Sweat Equity Treatment

**REC-030** Founder time and effort during pre-compensation periods:

- Recognized as contribution to project formation.
- May result in deferred compensation at appropriate levels.
- Does not justify ongoing claims on value created.

### 4.5 Reputation Recognition

**REC-040** Founder reputation lent to the project:

- Acknowledged in public communication during the founder period.
- Subject to the Public Communication Doctrine.
- Does not require continued public association after the founder period.

## 5. Founder Conduct

### 5.1 Conduct Standards

**COND-001** Founders meet conduct standards equivalent to senior leadership:

- Per Federation Membership Agreement §10.1.
- Per Personnel and Compensation Philosophy.
- With attention to the heightened symbolic weight of founder conduct.

### 5.2 Conflict of Interest

**COND-010** Founder conflicts of interest are managed through:

- Disclosure to Funding Committee, Stewardship Office, and Federation governance as applicable.
- Recusal from decisions where conflict exists.
- Annual conflict review.
- Transparent management when conflicts continue.

### 5.3 Outside Activities

**COND-020** Founders may engage in activities outside Robotanica during the founder period:

- Provided no conflict with Robotanica's interests.
- Provided no compromise of founder availability for Robotanica work.
- Disclosed to relevant governance.

### 5.4 Personal Brand Discipline

**COND-030** Founders manage their personal brand carefully:

- Acknowledging the project without overshadowing it.
- Avoiding "personality cult" patterns.
- Distinguishing personal views from institutional positions.
- Per Public Communication Doctrine.

### 5.5 Behavior Standards

**COND-040** Founders meet specific behavior standards:

- No harassment or abuse of personnel, partners, communities.
- No exploitation of founder authority for personal benefit.
- No retaliation against critics or whistleblowers.
- No coercion in funding, partnership, or governance contexts.

### 5.6 Conduct Failure

**COND-050** Where founder conduct falls below standards:

- Investigation per Personnel Philosophy §11 and Federation governance procedures.
- Consequences scaled to severity, including transition out of founder role.
- Public record reflects findings honestly.

## 6. Succession

### 6.1 Founder Period Endpoints

**SUC-001** The founder period ends:

- When the founder voluntarily transitions out.
- When governance structures are sufficiently developed that the founder role becomes redundant.
- After typically 3-7 years from project formation.
- Earlier if founder conduct or capacity warrants transition.

### 6.2 Succession Planning

**SUC-010** Succession planning begins early:

- Within the first year of the founder period, succession planning begins.
- Successor identification is governance, not founder appointment.
- Continuity of operations through transition is supported.

### 6.3 Founder Transition

**SUC-020** Founder transition is structured:

- Documented role transfer plan.
- Period of overlap where appropriate.
- Public communication of transition per Public Communication Doctrine.
- Continued engagement in alumni capacity if mutual.

### 6.4 No Permanent Founder Authority

**SUC-030** Founders accept that no founder authority persists permanently:

- After the founder period, former founders have no special authority.
- Former founders may speak publicly in personal capacity.
- Former founders may remain involved in advisory capacities if invited.

### 6.5 Founder Death or Incapacity

**SUC-040** Where founders depart through death or incapacity:

- Continuity of operations protected through governance structures.
- Founder estate or family interests treated per documented terms.
- No founder role transfers to family or designated successor without governance approval.

### 6.6 Founder Withdrawal

**SUC-050** Founders may withdraw from the project:

- With reasonable notice (typically 6 months minimum).
- With transition support per personnel transitions.
- Without retaining ongoing claims on the project.

### 6.7 Forced Transition

**SUC-060** Founders may be transitioned out by Federation governance:

- Where conduct warrants per §5.5.
- Where founder period has extended beyond reasonable bounds (typically 7 years).
- Where founder capacity or fit has materially changed.

The forced transition process includes due process, opportunity to respond, and decision by appropriate governance with appeal rights.

## 7. Multiple Founders

Most projects have multiple founders. Specific dynamics apply.

### 7.1 Equality Among Founders

**MULT-001** Multiple founders are equals in their founder commitments:

- All meet the same Founder Charter commitments.
- All receive the same recognition framework.
- No founder hierarchy beyond functional role differences.

### 7.2 Functional Roles

**MULT-010** Founders may have functional differences:

- One may lead operations, another fundraising, another scientific direction.
- Functional differences do not produce hierarchical differences.
- Compensation reflects role per Personnel Philosophy.

### 7.3 Disagreement Among Founders

**MULT-020** Founders may disagree on direction:

- Disagreements addressed through dialogue first.
- Persistent disagreements addressed through governance, not founder dominance.
- Founders accepting governance decisions even when disagreeing.

### 7.4 Co-Founder Departure

**MULT-030** Where one of multiple founders departs:

- Remaining founders continue per their commitments.
- Departing founder transitions per §6.3.
- Project continuity protected.

### 7.5 New Founder Addition

**MULT-040** Adding founders after initial formation:

- Subject to existing founders' approval and governance review.
- Documented through the same Founder Charter signing.
- Founder period for new founder calibrated to current project phase.

## 8. Founder Family and Personal Relationships

### 8.1 Family Members

**FAM-001** Founder family members:

- Are not given preferential roles in Robotanica.
- May participate in Robotanica through standard processes.
- Are subject to nepotism prevention measures.

### 8.2 Founder Personal Relationships

**FAM-010** Founder personal relationships within Robotanica (romantic partnerships, close friendships) are managed:

- Disclosure to relevant governance.
- Recusal from decisions where conflict exists.
- Power dynamics monitored.

### 8.3 Founder Family Capital

**FAM-020** Founder family capital invested in Robotanica:

- Treated under Funding Strategy disclosure rules.
- Subject to single-source dominance limits (per Funding Strategy §3.1).
- Not given preferential terms.

## 9. Founder Charter Adoption

### 9.1 Signing

**ADOPT-001** Founders sign the Founder Charter when entering the founder role:

- Signed upon project formation for initial founders.
- Signed upon entry for later-added founders.
- Signature recorded in public record.

### 9.2 Adaptation From Template

**ADOPT-010** Specific founders may adapt this template:

- Adding personal commitments consistent with the template.
- Adjusting specific provisions where genuine context warrants (e.g., founder compensation arrangements per Personnel Philosophy).
- Not weakening the template's core commitments.

### 9.3 Adaptation Approval

**ADOPT-020** Adapted Founder Charters are approved by:

- Federation governance (initial founders).
- Existing founders and Federation governance (later-added founders).

### 9.4 Public Record

**ADOPT-030** Signed Founder Charters are part of the public record:

- Identifying the founder.
- Listing the specific commitments.
- Recording the date of adoption and term.
- Updating with any amendments.

### 9.5 Amendment

**ADOPT-040** Adopted Founder Charters may be amended:

- By mutual agreement of founder and Federation governance.
- Subject to Charter compatibility.
- Recorded in public record.

## 10. The Honest Limitations

This template has honest limitations.

### 10.1 Founders Are Not Fully Constrained by Documents

**LIMIT-001** No document fully constrains human behavior. Founder commitments depend on the founders' own integrity and willingness to be bound. The Founder Charter creates accountability structures but cannot guarantee adherence.

### 10.2 Charisma and Reputation Effects

**LIMIT-010** Founders' charisma, reputation, and personal influence may exceed what the Charter formally grants. Structural defenses (term limits, governance distribution) are partial; the rest depends on founders' willingness to honor the spirit of bounded authority.

### 10.3 The Template May Need Revision

**LIMIT-020** This template is v0. Real founder situations will reveal gaps and require revisions. Specific founders may legitimately propose template changes that the federation governance reviews.

### 10.4 Cultural Variation

**LIMIT-030** Founder commitments may have cultural variation. A template developed in one cultural register may need translation for founders from other contexts. Adaptation is welcome where it preserves substance.

### 10.5 Founder Departure Is Not Always Clean

**LIMIT-040** Founder departures sometimes involve conflict, public disagreement, or disputed circumstances. The Charter cannot eliminate these dynamics; it can only structure how they are managed.

### 10.6 The Project Survives Founders

**LIMIT-050** The most important commitment in this Founder Charter is that the project survives the founders. Specific founders are real and significant; the project transcends them. Founders accepting this is the deepest commitment.

## 11. Sample Provisions

The following provisions might appear in a specific founder's adapted Founder Charter:

**For a primary financial founder:**

- Documented capital contribution and terms.
- Bounded return on contribution per Personnel Philosophy §9.
- Specific transition timeline tied to capital deployment phases.
- Continued advisory engagement post-founder-period.

**For a primary technical founder:**

- Documented role in platform engineering.
- Transition of technical leadership to senior engineering team.
- Continued engagement in technical advisory capacity.
- Open-source commitments to architectural choices.

**For a primary partnerships founder:**

- Documented role in partnership formation.
- Transition of partnership management to Stewardship Office.
- Continued engagement in major partnership relationships.
- Specific community partnership transition arrangements.

These are illustrative; specific provisions depend on specific founders.

## 12. Open Questions

These are unresolved questions in the Founder Charter framework:

- **Specific founder identification.** This template is for unnamed founders. When founders are identified, the template adapts to their specific contributions and transitions.
- **Founder period length.** 3-7 years is approximate. Specific projects may warrant different lengths based on complexity, scale, and capacity development.
- **Founder compensation specifics.** While bounded by Personnel Philosophy §9, specific founder compensation requires adaptation per role and contribution.
- **Founder family business interests.** Where founders come from family enterprises with potential overlap, additional provisions are needed.
- **Founder attached organizations.** If founders represent existing organizations (foundations, NGOs), the relationship between those organizations and Robotanica needs specific handling.
- **Multiple founder coordination.** Specific decision-making procedures among founders during founder period need elaboration based on the actual founder cohort.
- **International founder cohort.** If founders span multiple jurisdictions and cultures, jurisdictional and cultural variation in commitments and expectations need adaptation.
- **Founder-as-Steward continuation.** The relationship between former founders' continued engagement as Stewards and their formal departure from founder roles requires specific provisions.
